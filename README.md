This repository contains the complete deliverables for the CSE0001 Digital Literacy Project, where I took on the role of a **Student Digital Ambassador**. ## Task Summaries

### Task 1: Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic

**Module:** Introduction to Digital Literacy

Created a single-page visual resource using **Canva** titled _"Navigating the Digital World"_ to help first-year students understand key digital literacy concepts. The infographic covers:

- What digital literacy means
- Safe internet practices (passwords, 2FA, phishing awareness)
- Building a professional online presence (GitHub, LinkedIn)
- Useful digital tools for students (Google Workspace, Notion)
- Email etiquette basics

**Key Learning:** Digital literacy is more about mindset than technical skill - understanding _why_ something matters changes how seriously we approach it.

**Module:** Digital Communication and Netiquette

**Part A - Professional Emails:**

- Drafted two professional emails following proper structure, tone, and etiquette
  1. Student to professor requesting assignment extension

### Task 5: Cybercrime Awareness and Prevention

**Module:** Cybersecurity and Online Safety

**Part A - Case Study:**

- Researched and documented UPI/Online Payment Fraud
- Explained how these scams work and why they're so effective

**Part B - Prevention Checklist:**

- Created a "Stay Safe Online" guide with 8+ actionable tips
- Included financial safety tips specific to UPI transactions
- Added reporting channels: National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) and Helpline 1930

**Key Learning:** Most UPI fraud doesn't involve complex hacking - it exploits users' misunderstanding of how transactions work. The gap between how UPI works and how people _think_ it works is where fraud happens.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Digital literacy is a continuous journey**, not a one-time learning experience
2. **Small habits matter**: How you write emails, verify transactions, and present yourself online all contribute to your digital footprint
3. **Professional presence starts early**: Building profiles now, even when minimal, creates a foundation for growth
4. **Security is about awareness**: Understanding _why_ practices exist (like 2FA) makes you take them more seriously
5. **Tools are enablers**: The right digital tools can significantly enhance both learning and collaboration
