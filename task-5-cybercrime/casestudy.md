# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI fraud is one of the most common types of cybercrime today, especially among students and young users who rely heavily on digital payments. Unlike what many people assume, these scams usually don’t involve breaking into systems - they rely on tricking the user.

A typical scenario starts with a message or call from someone posing as a buyer, delivery agent, or even a bank representative. For example, a student trying to sell something online might get a message from a “buyer” who claims to have sent the payment. The scammer then shares a QR code or a payment request and insists that the user needs to scan it or approve it to receive the money.

At this point, the victim believes they are receiving funds, but in reality, they are authorizing a payment. The moment they enter their UPI PIN, the money is transferred out of their account.

Students are often targeted because they are active on platforms like marketplaces and social media, and may not fully understand how UPI transactions actually work.

The consequences are immediate - loss of money, panic, and in many cases, little chance of recovery. What makes this more concerning is how simple the trick is, yet how effective it continues to be.
